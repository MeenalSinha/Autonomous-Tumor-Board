# NCCN Guidelines Summary (Simulated)
# Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)

## Version 2.2026

### Treatment by Stage

#### Stage I (T1-2, N0, M0)
**Preferred:**
- Surgical resection (lobectomy with lymph node dissection)
- Category 1 evidence

**Alternative (medically inoperable):**
- SBRT (Stereotactic Body Radiation Therapy)
- Category 1 for peripheral tumors
- Conventional RT for central tumors

**Systemic Therapy:**
- Adjuvant chemotherapy for high-risk Stage I (tumor >4cm)
- Consider adjuvant immunotherapy in PD-L1+ tumors

#### Stage II (T3 or N1, M0)
**Preferred:**
- Surgical resection + adjuvant chemotherapy
- Category 1 evidence

**Adjuvant Therapy:**
- Platinum-based doublet chemotherapy (4 cycles)
- Consider adjuvant immunotherapy (atezolizumab) for PD-L1 ≥1%

#### Stage III (T4 or N2-3, M0)
**Resectable:**
- Neoadjuvant chemoimmunotherapy → Surgery → Adjuvant therapy
- Category 2A

**Unresectable:**
- Concurrent chemoradiation → Consolidation immunotherapy (durvalumab)
- Category 1 for PD-L1+ patients

#### Stage IV (M1)
**First-Line Systemic Therapy:**

For PD-L1 ≥50%:
- Pembrolizumab monotherapy (Category 1)
- OR Pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (Category 1)

For PD-L1 1-49%:
- Pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (Category 1)
- OR Atezolizumab + chemotherapy + bevacizumab (Category 1)

For PD-L1 <1%:
- Chemotherapy ± bevacizumab
- OR Chemotherapy + immunotherapy

**Molecular Testing Required:**
- EGFR
- ALK
- ROS1
- BRAF V600E
- NTRK
- MET exon 14 skipping
- RET
- KRAS G12C
- HER2

### Biomarker-Directed Therapy

#### EGFR Mutations
- Osimertinib (preferred, Category 1)
- Afatinib or Erlotinib (alternative)

#### ALK Rearrangements
- Alectinib (preferred, Category 1)
- Brigatinib or Lorlatinib (alternative)

#### PD-L1 Expression
- ≥50%: Strong indication for immunotherapy
- 1-49%: Consider combination therapy
- <1%: Limited benefit from immunotherapy monotherapy

### Special Considerations

**Elderly Patients (>75 years):**
- Comprehensive geriatric assessment recommended
- Consider single-agent chemotherapy or reduced doses
- Immunotherapy acceptable if performance status adequate

**Brain Metastases:**
- SRS (Stereotactic Radiosurgery) for ≤4 lesions
- Whole brain RT for multiple lesions
- Targeted therapy penetrates blood-brain barrier (osimertinib, alectinib)

**Performance Status:**
- ECOG 0-1: Fit for aggressive multimodal therapy
- ECOG 2: Consider less intensive regimens
- ECOG ≥3: Best supportive care or palliative RT

### Surveillance

**Post-Resection:**
- History & physical every 3-6 months for 2 years, then annually
- CT chest with contrast every 6-12 months for 2 years, then annually
- Low-dose CT for high-risk patients
